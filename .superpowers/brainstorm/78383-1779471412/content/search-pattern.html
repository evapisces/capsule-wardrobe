A — Command Palette

Full-screen overlay, search-first. You type immediately — results filter as you go. Keyboard-native (⌘K to open, arrows to navigate, Enter to add). Think Notion, Linear, Raycast. Best for power users and desktop. Feels fast and modern.

B — Bottom Sheet

Slides up from the bottom, partially covering the screen. Standard on iOS/Android, increasingly common on desktop web too. Drag to expand for more results. Keeps context visible above. Best for touch + photo-heavy browsing.

C — Anchored Popover

A compact floating panel that appears right next to the "+" button — stays spatially connected to what triggered it. No overlay, no full-screen takeover. Great on desktop. On mobile it can feel cramped unless it auto-expands to a sheet.

My take: B (bottom sheet) handles photos best and works across both desktop and mobile. A is the cleanest for keyboard-heavy users. C is a nice desktop micro-interaction but degrades on small screens. Let me know in the terminal.
